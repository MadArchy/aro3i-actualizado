🔬 The Science

WHY THE GUMLINE
CHANGES EVERYTHING.

Most delivery routes — your cheeks, tongue, even your gut — are protected by thick, tightly sealed tissue designed to keep things out. The Junctional Epithelium (JE) is different. It's a thin, naturally "leaky" tissue at the very base of the gingival sulcus — the tiny groove where your gum meets your tooth.

Think of it as the body's hidden gate. Unlike the thick barrier of your cheek lining, the JE has wide intercellular spaces and minimal tight junctions — making it exceptionally permeable to molecules placed directly into the sulcus.

When ARO Floss deposits vitamins into that groove, they absorb straight through the JE into richly vascularized connective tissue — entering your bloodstream without ever touching your stomach. Published in Nature Biomedical Engineering, 2026.

🔬 The Science

THE HIDDEN GATE
INSIDE YOUR MOUTH.

Your mouth is not a uniform surface. The cheek lining and tongue are thick, tightly sealed barriers — designed by evolution to keep things out. But at the very base of the gingival sulcus sits a completely different tissue: the Junctional Epithelium (JE).

The JE is thin, non-keratinized, and has naturally wide intercellular spaces — making it a high-permeability "open gate." It's also surrounded by rich vasculature and lymphatic drainage, meaning whatever gets through goes directly into systemic circulation.

ARO Pick's precision-tapered strip is engineered specifically to reach this tissue — 1 to 3mm deep into the sulcus. At that depth, vitamins are shielded from saliva washout and salivary enzymes, absorbed directly where it counts. Published in Nature Biomedical Engineering, 2026.
